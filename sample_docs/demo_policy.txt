ACME Corporation — Employee Leave Policy
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Version: 2.1

1. Annual Leave
All full-time employees are entitled to 21 days of paid annual leave per calendar year. Part-time employees receive leave proportional to their working hours. Annual leave must be approved by the direct manager at least 5 business days in advance. Unused leave can be carried over to the next year, up to a maximum of 10 days.

2. Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 12 days of paid sick leave per year. A medical certificate from a registered physician is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days. The Human Resources department must be notified within 2 hours of the start of the work day.

3. Parental Leave
New parents are eligible for 16 weeks of paid parental leave. This applies to both primary and secondary caregivers. Parental leave must be requested at least 30 days before the expected date. The employee's position is guaranteed upon return.

4. Remote Work Policy
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week with manager approval. Remote work arrangements must be documented in the HR system. Employees working remotely must be available during core hours (10 AM to 4 PM). The IT department provides necessary equipment for remote work setups.

5. Professional Development Leave
Each employee is entitled to 5 days per year for professional development activities. This includes attending conferences, workshops, and certification programs. Requests must be submitted to the Learning and Development team. Expenses up to $2000 per year are covered by the company.

6. Bereavement Leave
Employees are granted 5 days of paid bereavement leave for the death of an immediate family member. Immediate family includes spouse, children, parents, and siblings. An additional 2 days may be granted for travel if the funeral is more than 500 miles away.

7. Approval Authority
Department managers can approve leave requests up to 5 consecutive days. Leave requests exceeding 5 days require approval from the VP of the department. Extended leave beyond 30 days requires approval from the Chief Human Resources Officer, Sarah Thompson. All leave records are maintained by the HR Operations team led by Michael Chen.
